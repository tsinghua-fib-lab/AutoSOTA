## What Worked
- **SGD Momentum (0.9) for post-unlearning phase**: The baseline used `momentum=0` (plain SGD) during the post-unlearning fine-tuning. Adding standard momentum of 0.9 dramatically smoothed the oscillating per-epoch accuracy (which previously swung 30-47% in epochs 5-20) and enabled monotonic convergence. This is the single most impactful change.
- **Combined with higher LR**: The momentum + LR combination worked synergistically. The baseline with LR=0.2 alone (iter 2) still showed 22 epochs; momentum alone (not tested) likely would also improve; together they create a step-change improvement.
- The improvement is robust: confirmed in both iter 3 test and the final evaluation.

## What Didn't Work
- **Increasing unlearn max_lr (IDEA-007)**: Setting unlearn LR to 0.05 caused catastrophic model collapse. The PABI sigma_t formula has `eta_t * weight_decay` in the decay factor: `decay_factor = 1 - eta_t * lambda_t`. With LR=0.05 and WD=50, `decay_factor = 1 - 0.05*50 = -1.5`, which is invalid/unstable. **CRITICAL CONSTRAINT**: `unlearn_lr * unlearn_weight_decay < 1` must hold.
- **Higher LR alone (0.2 vs 0.1)**: Without momentum, the higher post_unlearn LR did not reduce the 50% threshold. The model still reached 50% at epoch 22, though final accuracy improved.

## Root Cause Analysis
The baseline used `momentum=0` (vanilla SGD without momentum) for post-unlearning. This is unusual — standard ML practice always uses momentum ≥ 0.9 for SGD. Without momentum, the optimizer makes noisy, uncorrelated steps, causing the severe epoch-to-epoch accuracy oscillations (e.g., epoch 12: 41%, epoch 13: 44%, epoch 14: 39%). Momentum smooths these into monotonic improvement.
